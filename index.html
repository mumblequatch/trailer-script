NARRATOR

Once upon a time, there was a kid named Benjamin. He loved reading and hanging out with his friends, playing video games. Maybe a kid like you.

And he liked his parents. They crazy-danced to music, made videos, and went to the library a lot.

And yet, there was something that crept up on him in quiet moments and made him feel so sad that he felt like he was made of sadness. And so lonely he felt he would never feel good again. This something was that no matter how great his life was, no matter how fun his friends were or supporting and loving his parents could be, he never completely felt like this life was his. He never felt like he truly belonged here.

But one morning, just after Benjamin asked his dad to pass the maple syrup, the air above the breakfast table seemed to tear apart, and through the hole emerged giant, clawed hands on the ends of long, multi-jointed grey arms. As Benjamin screamed, the clawed hands grabbed his parents and yanked them through, and just as quickly as it had appeared, the hole was gone.

“What the hell…?” Benjamin thought.

And in a way, he was right. For as he would soon learn, his parents had been taken to the Underworld. A vast country populated by all one hundred and ten billion people who have ever lived and died. All the eras of history, intermingled like sediment at the bottom of an infinite ocean. Both unthinkably large and, to the dead, unbearably small.

Before he was born, Benjamin’s parents gave up everything to protect him from this place. And now, it is into this same dark country that he must venture, alone, if he wants to save them.

Along the way, he will forge unlikely friendships. He will be hunted by a ruler who fears what his family represents. He will find himself at the center of a revolution among the dead.

DIEGO

Kid, we’ve been fighting this thing for a hundred years and getting nowhere. Having you on our side would be, let’s just say, a distinct advantage.

NARRATOR

And he will find out what his parents worked so hard to hide from him: himself.

I do not believe in magic. And yet I must believe in you.
